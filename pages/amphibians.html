Amphibians

Amphibians are cold-blooded animals that typically live both on land and in water.

Featured Amphibian

Axolotl

Axolotl is neoteny, a condition where they never grow up in the traditional sense. They retain their larval, or "tadpole-like," features and remain fully aquatic throughout their entire lives, never undergoing metamorphosis to become land-dwelling like many of their close relatives.

Common Amphibians

Fire Salamander

Fire Salamanders species give birth to live, fully developed larvae directly into the water.

Toad

Toads have dry, bumpy skin unlike frogs.

Olm

The Olm is also known as the "human fish" due to its fleshy skin color.

Newt

Newts have indefinite regenerative powers.

Caecilian

Caecilians are legless and resemble worms.

Chinese Giant Salamander

Chinese Giant Salamander is the world's largest amphibian.

Red-eyed tree Frog

Red-eyed tree frogs possess a specialized third eyelid to stay camouflaged while still detecting movement when asleep.

Wallace’s flying frog

Wallace's flying frog uses its massive, webbed feet as natural parachutes to glide over rainforest canopy.

Glass Frog

You can see the Glass Frog's organs through their skin.
