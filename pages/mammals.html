Mammals

Mammals are warm-blooded vertebrate animals characterized by the presence of hair and the ability to produce milk for their young.

Featured Mammal

Dog

Dogs are loyal companions and have an excellent sense of smell. They can detect scents that humans cannot, making them invaluable in search and rescue operations.

Common Mammals

Cat

Small, sleek body, covered in soft fur that comes in many colours like white, black, grey, brown, and cream.

Monkey

The presence of a tail (even if only a tiny nub), along with their narrow-chested bodies and other features of the skeleton, distinguishes monkeys from apes.

Cow

Cows are herbivorous mammals with cloven hoofs and two horns.

Lion

Lions have strong, compact bodies and powerful forelegs, teeth and jaws for pulling down and killing prey.

Horse

A large, four-legged animal with hooves, a long nose and tail, and a mane of hair along its upper back.

Tiger

Tigers are the largest cats in the world, known for their distinctive orange coats with black stripes.

Sheep

Domestic sheep are relatively small ruminants, usually with a crimped hair called wool and often with horns forming a lateral spiral.

Goat

Goats are small ruminants with a distinctive beard and often horns.

Elephant

Elephants are the largest land animals on Earth, known for their intelligence and complex social structures.
