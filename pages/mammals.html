Mammals

Mammals are warm-blooded vertebrate animals characterized by the presence of hair and the ability to produce milk for their young.

Featured Mammal

Dog

Dogs can detect diseases like cancer or low blood sugar with their incredible sense of smell.

Common Mammals

Cat

Cats have 32 muscles in each ear, allowing them to rotate and hear even the tiniest sounds.

Monkey

Some monkeys use tools like sticks to extract insects or crack open nuts.

Cow

Cows have almost 360-degree vision and can remember faces of other cows for years.

Lion

A lion’s roar can be heard up to 5 miles away.

Horse

Horses can sleep both lying down and standing up, thanks to a special “stay apparatus” in their legs.

Tiger

Tigers have striped skin under their fur — no two tigers have the same pattern.

Sheep

Sheep have excellent memories and can remember at least 50 individual faces for years.

Goat

Goats have rectangular pupils, giving them a wide field of vision to spot predators.

Elephant

Elephants can recognize themselves in mirrors, showing a high level of self-awareness.
