Reptiles

A reptile is a cold-blooded, scale-covered vertebrate that breathes air and typically lays tough, leathery eggs, relying on the environment rather than its own body to stay warm.

Featured Reptile

Leopard Gecko

Leopard Gecko (Eublepharis macularius): Famous for its smiling face and plump tail, this is one of the few geckos that can actually blink at you with tiny eyelids.

Reptiles

Arabian Sand Boa

Arabian Sand Boa (Eryx jayakari): This goofy snake spends its life buried in the dunes with only its top-mounted eyes peeking out like a cartoon character.

Komodo Dragon

Komodo Dragon (Varanus komodoensis): As the world's largest lizard, this island giant is a legendary apex predator equipped with shark-like teeth and a venomous bite.

Pan's Box Turtle

Pan's Box Turtle (Cuora pani): This ultra-rare turtle is famous for its hinged belly shell that lets it lock itself inside like a high-security box.

Eyelash Viper

Eyelash Viper (Bothriechis schlegelii): This stunning snake looks like a jungle jewel thanks to its vibrant colors and the unique, lash-like scales above its eyes.

Western Hognose Snake

Western Hognose Snake (Heterodon nasicus): Between its adorable shovel-shaped nose and its habit of dramatically playing dead, this snake is the ultimate drama queen.

Union Island Gecko

Union Island Gecko (Gonatodes daudini): Roughly the size of a paperclip, this incredibly rare gecko is prized for neon markings that look like they belong in a sci-fi movie.

King Cobra

King Cobra (Ophiophagus hannah): The undisputed king of serpents, this famous reptile is the world's longest venomous snake and eats almost nothing but other snakes.

Pig-Nosed Turtle

Pig-Nosed Turtle (Carettochelys insculpta): This adorable freshwater oddity looks like a hybrid creature featuring a pig-like snout and flippers usually only seen on sea turtles.

Asian Tiger Snake

Asian Tiger Snake (Rhabdophis tigrinus): This rare species is a biological marvel that is both venomous to bite and poisonous to touch because it steals toxins from the toads it eats.
